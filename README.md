# FMCG-Report

# Sales Performance Dashboard (2010–2011)

## Project Overview

This project presents an **interactive Sales Performance Dashboard** built to analyze sales data for a beverage distribution business across multiple states, customers, products, and sales representatives between **2010 and 2011**.

The dashboard provides insights into **revenue performance, product sales, regional distribution, and salesperson contribution**, enabling stakeholders to quickly understand business performance and identify opportunities for growth.

Key metrics visualized include:

* **Total Revenue:** ₦2,152,454
* **Total Quantity Sold:** 7,997 units
* **Revenue by Year**
* **Revenue by Month**
* **Revenue by State**
* **Top 5 Salespersons by Revenue**
* Interactive filters for **Date, Product, Customer, and State**

---

# Problem Statement

Sales data existed across multiple dimensions such as **products, customers, states, and sales representatives**, but decision-makers lacked a centralized and visual system to answer key business questions:

* Which **states generate the most revenue?**
* Who are the **top-performing salespeople?**
* Which **months drive the highest sales performance?**
* How does **yearly performance compare over time?**
* Which **products and customers contribute most to revenue?**

Without a clear visualization layer, it was difficult for management to **identify trends, measure performance, and make informed strategic decisions**.

---

# Methodology

## 1. Data Preparation

* Collected and structured sales transaction data.
* Cleaned the dataset to ensure:

  * consistent product naming
  * accurate revenue calculations
  * valid dates and quantities
* Created calculated fields using pivot tables and pivot charts such as:

  * Total Revenue
  * Sales by Month
  * Sales by State
  * Salesperson performance metrics

## 2. Data Modeling

The dataset was organized into key analytical dimensions:

* **Time Dimension:** Year and Month
* **Product Dimension:** Coca-Cola, Fanta, Eva, Five Alive, Sprite
* **Customer Dimension:** Retail partners such as KFC, Shoprite, Mr Biggs, etc.
* **Geographical Dimension:** Lagos, Kano, Kaduna, Enugu
* **Sales Representative Dimension**

These relationships enabled flexible filtering and cross-analysis.

## 3. Dashboard Development

The dashboard was designed to provide both **high-level insights and drill-down capability** using:

* KPI Cards for key performance metrics
* Bar charts for comparative analysis
* Line charts for trend analysis
* Interactive slicers for user-driven exploration

The design prioritizes **clarity, quick insight discovery, and executive usability**.

# Impact

This dashboard provides a **single source of truth for sales performance**, enabling stakeholders to:

* Monitor revenue performance quickly
* Identify top-performing regions and salespeople
* Understand seasonal sales patterns
* Track product and customer contributions
* Make **data-driven strategic decisions**

The solution improves **reporting efficiency, transparency, and decision-making speed**.

---

# Recommendations

### 1. Strengthen High-Performing Markets

Since **Kano and Lagos dominate revenue**, the company should:

* Increase product availability
* Strengthen distribution channels
* Invest in targeted marketing campaigns.

### 2. Support Lower-Performing Regions

States such as **Kaduna and Enugu** may benefit from:

* improved sales coverage
* promotional incentives
* customer acquisition strategies.

### 3. Replicate Top Salesperson Strategies

Analyze the techniques used by **top-performing sales representatives** and share best practices across the sales team.

### 4. Leverage Seasonal Sales Opportunities

The spike in **December sales** suggests strong holiday demand. The company should:

* increase inventory
* run seasonal promotions
* launch bundled offers.

### 5. Improve Product-Level Analytics

Further analysis can identify **top-performing products per region**, enabling better inventory and marketing decisions.
